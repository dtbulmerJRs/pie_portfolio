    Modders Workshop: An Introduction to creating custom content in Civilization 4

    Team: Craig, David, and Derrik.

    What: Modder's Workshop is an attempt to show that creating addons for games is a great activity for those interested in game design. A series of short demos will be prepared for the College of Engineering organizations, or any one from any field curious about how making your own content for games is a great way to learn game development.

    Where: ECS 304 ( the meeting near EAT's office ) When: Tuesdays at 2:00 P.M. throughout Fall 2011 semester.

    What you can do to prepare: Since you will need blender for the demo, please see the link for installing Blender 2.49b, Nifskope, Python,and PyFFi and follow the instuctions ( since the link was from the modding community for Oblivion, they will tell you to link your Nifskope to the game; you can ignore those instructions )

    Another demo will show how programmers can make addons through programming, and the links are listed below for wome great examples of modding tutorials. Next week we will have a custom tutorial involving the programming languages C++, Python, and Lua; you will also be introduced to XML and the DAT file. For that, it might be handy to have a programming IDE such as Visual Studio or a free open source IDE such as Netbeans.

    Demos in the future will expand on what was covered in the previous demos.
